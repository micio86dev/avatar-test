# Internationalization (i18n)

Internationalization is a design decision, not a late-stage feature. Retrofitting
it into an application that hardcoded its assumptions is expensive and error-prone.

When applicable:

- Design applications with internationalization in mind from the start.
- Avoid hardcoded user-facing strings. Route them through a translation layer,
  message catalog, or resource bundle.
- Keep user-facing text separate from logic so copy can change without touching code.
- Prefer an architecture that allows future localization without major refactoring,
  even if only one locale ships today.

## Beyond strings

Localization is more than translating text. When relevant, account for:

- Dates, times, time zones, and calendars.
- Numbers, currencies, and units.
- Pluralization and gender rules, which vary by language.
- Text direction (LTR / RTL) and layout mirroring.
- Collation and sorting order.

## Not always required

For internal tools, single-locale products, or throwaway prototypes, full i18n may be
unnecessary. Even then, avoid choices that would make future localization a rewrite —
keep strings out of deeply nested logic and avoid concatenating sentence fragments.
